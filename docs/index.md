## What is HDRL?

The **Health Data Readiness Level (HDRL)** Assessment Framework is a comprehensive maturity model designed to evaluate the readiness of health data organisations and systems to participate in the UK Health Data Research Service (HDRS).

Developed through systematic evidence synthesis of **56 global frameworks** (including WHO, OECD, FAIR, CMMI, and HIMSS), HDRL fills the "missing middle" between high-level principles (Five Safes, Alliance Principles) and technical specifications (SATRE, NHS SDE Specs, ISO 27001).

!!! info "Not a pass/fail audit"
    HDRL is a **roadmap for investment and growth**. It supports planning, not accreditation. Participation decisions are made through separate governance processes.

## Who is it for?

| Audience | What HDRL provides |
|:---------|:-------------------|
| **Policy Makers** | Pinpoints exactly where investment is needed (e.g., Legislation vs Compute) |
| **Research Services** | A clear "Licence to Operate" checklist and improvement roadmap |
| **Industry Partners** | Guarantees that a "Level 4" partner delivers predictability and speed |
| **Funders** | Evidence-based framework for allocation decisions across the 3-year cycle |

## Key Features

- **Scientifically rigorous** — Synthesised from 56 global frameworks (WHO, OECD, FAIR, CMMI)
- **Roadmap-based** — Not a pass/fail audit, but a guide for investment and growth
- **Outcome-focused** — Measures if you can *deliver*, not just if you have a policy
- **UK-wide** — Designed for the devolved nations with cross-border considerations
- **Modular** — Baseline Core, Capability modules, and Optional indicators
